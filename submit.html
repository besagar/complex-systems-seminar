Become a Speaker

Share your expertise in complex systems theory through our chalkboard seminar format. We welcome talks on foundational methods, seminal results, and theoretical frameworks.

Talk Guidelines

Format Expectations

  • Duration: 75-90 minutes presentation + 30-45 minutes discussion
  • Style: Chalkboard presentation (no slides)
  • Depth: Focus on theoretical foundations and mathematical rigor
  • Audience: Graduate students, postdocs, and faculty

Preferred Topics

  • Field-theoretic techniques in non-equilibrium systems
  • Replica theory and glassy systems
  • Modern aspects of stochastic processes and random walks
  • Modern continuum physics
  • Complex networks theory
  • Active matter and collective behavior
  • Other theoretical topics relevant to complex systems

What Makes a Good Talk

  • Clear mathematical exposition building from first principles
  • Connection to seminal papers and foundational results
  • Emphasis on theoretical understanding over recent results
  • Preparation for extended Q&A and discussion

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can present?

Graduate students, postdocs, and faculty from any department are welcome. We particularly encourage participation from physics, mathematics, chemistry, and life sciences researchers working on theoretical problems.

How much preparation is needed?

Expect to spend significant time preparing a 75-90 minute chalkboard presentation. The format requires careful organization and deep understanding of the material to facilitate meaningful discussion.

Can I present recent unpublished work?

While we welcome cutting-edge research, our focus is on foundational understanding and established theoretical methods. Consider framing new results within the context of seminal approaches and mathematical frameworks.

What about academic credit?

Students can receive academic credit for consistent participation and contribution to the seminar, similar to practices in other departments. Discuss this option with your advisor and indicate interest in your submission.
