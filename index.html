About Our Seminar

We are establishing a space for serious theoretical discussion and long-format presentations centered around the rich variety of topics in complex systems. This seminar differs markedly in tone, structure, and content from existing departmental seminars.

While traditional seminars tend toward short, slide-based presentations focusing on fresh and often still-developing results‚Äîmore akin to conference talks‚Äîour seminar aims to revive the tradition of deeper, dialogue-oriented sessions incorporating seminal results and methods of the complex systems field.

Our format is based on chalkboard presentations with significant time dedicated to discussions, creating an environment for careful, critical theoretical work among students.

Research Areas & Topics

We explore domains and approaches that are underrepresented in current curricula, focusing on theoretical foundations and mathematical rigor.

Current Series: Active Matter Theory

Our inaugural series (4-5 sessions) explores the theoretical foundations of active matter, from microscopic models to hydrodynamic descriptions.

Session 1-2: Active Brownian Motion

Different versions of active Brownian particle models, their mathematical formulation, and connections to experimental realizations in biological and synthetic systems.

Introductory Stochastic Processes

Session 3: The Vicsek Model

Discrete flocking models, phase transitions in collective motion, and the emergence of ordered states from local alignment interactions.

Introductory Phase Transitions

Session 4-5: Toner-Tu Theory

Hydrodynamic description of flocking, symmetry considerations, and dynamical renormalization group analysis of the long-wavelength effective theory.

Advanced Field Theory DRG
